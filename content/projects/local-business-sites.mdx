## Overview

Most small businesses in Cache Valley have either no website or a broken one.
That's a real market, and serving it is how I'm building web development
skills with actual stakes attached — real clients, real deadlines, real
"the contact form has to work" requirements.

This isn't a tutorial project. It's a small business: identify prospects on
Google Maps, run cold outreach by phone and email, build the site, and handle
domains and hosting. The first complete demo — a lawn care company site — is
built and in outreach.

## The Product

A five-section brochure site engineered for the actual use case: a customer
Googles the business, decides in ten seconds whether it's legitimate, and
calls.

- Sticky navigation with mobile hamburger menu
- Photo hero with overlay and a single clear call to action
- Services grid, social proof (real Google reviews), and service-area sections
- Quote request form wired to email via Formspree
- Fully responsive, tested mobile-first — because local search traffic is overwhelmingly mobile

## Engineering Decisions

- **Zero frameworks.** Pure HTML/CSS/JS in a single deployable file. A 5-page brochure site doesn't need React — it needs to load instantly on a phone with two bars of signal. Choosing *not* to use a framework was the engineering decision.
- **Static hosting.** Netlify/GitHub Pages: zero hosting cost, no server to maintain, nothing to get hacked.
- **Formspree for forms.** The one dynamic requirement, solved with a service instead of a backend — right-sized infrastructure.

## The Business Side

The technical build is the easy half. The other half is the skill most
engineering students never touch: outreach. Prospecting via Google Maps for
businesses with no web presence, first-contact phone calls, personalized
cold email, and a tracking system for every prospect and follow-up.

Deliberately practicing the uncomfortable skill — talking to strangers about
money — because engineering careers are built on communication as much as
computation.
